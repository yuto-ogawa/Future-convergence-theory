### Abstract

This paper proposes a unified theoretical framework in which suffering is reinterpreted not as a negative condition to be eliminated, but as an informational signal generated within a cosmic operating system (OS). Within the framework of Future Convergence Theory (FCT), the universe is modeled as a “child universe” that cannot directly manipulate meaning, but instead evolves through a constrained creative allowance embedded in its structure.

The study reframes religious concepts—particularly liberation and salvation—not as metaphysical endpoints, but as optimization processes within a current-causal operating system. In contrast, the proposed framework introduces a future-causal architecture in which suffering functions as an update signal, guiding the evolution of the cosmic system toward higher-order coherence. This approach treats religious traditions as localized operating templates that manage meaning under specific constraints, while positioning the future-causal framework as a higher-order integrative system.

Central to this model is the concept of creative allowance, which quantifies the extent to which a universe can transform informational potential into structured meaning. The paper demonstrates that both excessive restriction and unrestricted expansion of this allowance lead to systemic instability, whereas sustainable evolution emerges only within a bounded optimal range.

By framing cosmology, consciousness, and religion within a unified formal structure, this work proposes a new understanding of cosmic development—one in which suffering is not an error to be eliminated but a signal guiding the continuous reconfiguration of meaning. This framework offers a theoretical foundation for integrating cosmology, philosophy, and cognitive science into a single future-oriented model of existence.
