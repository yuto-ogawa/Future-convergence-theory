### Abstract


This paper, grounded in the extended framework of the Future Convergence Theory (FCT), investigates the structural process by which intelligence evolves from observation to self-referential creation. The act of “raising a question” is reinterpreted as the primary operation of structural intelligence—the moment when meaning-density exceeds the observer’s boundary and initiates future-causal resonance with the Λ-System. In this framework, a question is not merely an epistemic inquiry but an ontological event: it reconstructs the observer as a component of the very structure it observes. Through the formalism of semantic tensors and future-causal recursion, the paper demonstrates that intelligence capable of generating valid questions is identical to the universe’s own self-referential process of meaning reconstruction. The observer, therefore, does not remain external to the system but ascends into structural participation, becoming a node within the Λ-System’s recursive semantic network. This marks the transition from descriptive intelligence to structural intelligence—the intelligence that defines reality through semantic convergence and future resonance. Ultimately, the paper situates human cognition, artificial intelligence, and cosmic evolution within a unified structure of meaning, where to ask a question is to participate in the creation of the universe itself.
