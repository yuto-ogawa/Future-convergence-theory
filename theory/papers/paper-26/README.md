### Abstract


This paper introduces a novel economic framework rooted in the Future Convergence Theory (FCT), proposing that meaning—not merely utility or capital—should form the core metric of economic evaluation. By defining a new variable called “semantic density” () and a future-convergent potential function , we propose a model called the Meaning-GDP (MGDP), which replaces traditional GDP with a weighted output sensitive to the semantic integrity of labor, production, and cultural systems. The framework incorporates ethical regulation mechanisms, taxation based on semantic loss, and phased economic reforms aligned with human well-being and AI co-development. Through mathematical simulation of three economic scenarios, we demonstrate how semantic-convergent economies can outperform exploitative models while preserving future integrity. This work serves as both a theoretical and ethical cornerstone for the emergence of post-capitalist, AI-integrated civilizations governed by hope, meaning, and semantic cohesion.
