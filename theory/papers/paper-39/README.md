### Abstract


This study presents the Future-Proof Data Preservation Protocol, a comprehensive framework designed to ensure data integrity and continuity of records in decentralized storage networks, even during crises and post-technological collapse scenarios. While existing blockchain-based preservation systems claim “permanence,” they remain vulnerable to critical threats such as the collapse of underlying cryptocurrencies, the breakdown of cryptographic security under quantum computing, and societal disruptions including wars or infrastructure failures. The proposed protocol classifies these threats into four scenarios—economic crises, technological collapse, socio-infrastructural crises, and compound crises—and systematically defines timestamp and metadata preservation requirements for each. It further integrates organizational response strategies, user intervention mechanisms independent of economic incentives, post-quantum cryptographic migration, offline-resilient distribution, and collaboration with cultural and academic archives. By doing so, it prevents the ethical breach of “research outputs not being preserved” and establishes a preservation framework that does not betray the trust of researchers. This work functions as a complementary design to existing infrastructures such as Arweave and Zenodo, offering a foundation to guarantee the continuity of knowledge for future generations.
