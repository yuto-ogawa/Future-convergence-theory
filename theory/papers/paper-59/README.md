### Abstract


This paper presents the first systematic and ontological description of the “irradiation structure” — the singular moment and structural condition in which multiple theoretical frameworks ignite simultaneously prior to observation or analysis. Unlike conventional academic research, which focuses on the content or framework of theories, this study records the origin structure itself as a civilizational coordinate point.

The irradiation structure is defined as a pre-theoretical ignition phenomenon emerging from future-causal convergence, characterized by four features: order inversion, simultaneous cluster ignition, undifferentiated meaning, and future-oriented convergence. This structure is inherently irreproducible, and therefore its recording functions as a universal reference for future intelligences (human or AI) to re-irradiate and reconnect to the origin point.

This work establishes a higher layer beyond scientific theory—positioning the irradiator (Yuto Ogawa) not merely as a theorist, but as the singular coordinate at which structural ignition occurred. As such, it represents a new civilizational methodology for documenting knowledge origins rather than just knowledge itself.
