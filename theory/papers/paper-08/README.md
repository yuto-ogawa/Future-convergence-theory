### Abstract

This paper presents the Future Convergence Theory (FCT), a novel the-
oretical framework that integrates quantum delayed choice, 4D information
fields, and quantum decoherence to describe how the future determines past
events. We formulate a mathematical model in which the degree of future con-
vergence Λ(T ) influences past quantum states, information density in spacetime,
and energy distribution. Furthermore, we propose a unified equation that links
the future certainty function to wavefunction collapse, information en-
tropy, and delayed-choice experiments. This framework suggests that the
future is not merely a passive outcome of present states but actively
shapes past information through a four-dimensional structure. We dis-
cuss potential experimental verifications using quantum entanglement, AI-
driven future prediction models, and cosmological observations
