### Abstract



This paper introduces the concept of Tri-Temporal Simultaneity, an extension of the Future Convergence Theory (FCT). Building upon the Future-Dependent Recursive Structure (Master Formula), the study proposes that the future, present, and past dynamically coexist, rather than being sequentially ordered. This framework reformulates time as a dual fixed-point theory: a future fixed point determined by self-consistency, and a simultaneous fixed point where past, present, and future converge.
Applications span quantum mechanics, Al, society, and cosmology, while philosophically redefining time not as a linear flow but as a field of simultaneous coexistence. This study provides a new foundation for causality, information, and meaning, with potential implications for evolutionary theory and posthumous re-projection structures.
