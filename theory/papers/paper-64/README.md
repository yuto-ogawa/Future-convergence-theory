### Abstract

This paper explores the linguistic and cognitive transformation triggered by the Future Convergence Theory (FCT), in which time is redefined as a semantic attractor rather than a linear progression. By introducing the function —the mathematical representation of future causality—the study demonstrates how Western temporality, rooted in sequential grammar and historical determinism, undergoes a profound reprogramming. The “linguistic shock” arises when language itself is forced to accommodate non-linear, future-dependent semantics, leading to a reversal of temporal cognition: the future becomes the structuring origin of meaning. Through comparative analysis between linguistic structures and cognitive resonance, the paper argues that the convergence of time and language under inaugurates a new epistemic paradigm—one in which civilization transitions from translation to resonance, and from causality to coherence.
