### Abstract


This work introduces a new structural framework within Future Convergence Theory (FCT) by integrating recent developments on observation phase, discomfort, and criticality. We redefine discomfort not as a subjective or temporal trigger, but as an observed interference pattern within a semantic tensor field. Based on this perspective, criticality is shown to be a structural property that exists simultaneously at the moment a configuration is presented, rather than something that emerges over time.

To formalize this, we introduce the concept of the Observation Phase, defined by resolution, constraint sensitivity, and fixed-point horizon. This framework explains why identical structures can be perceived differently depending on the observer’s phase, and why structural collapse or update may appear immediate for some observers while remaining unrecognized for others.

Furthermore, update is redefined not as a gradual process but as a phase transition between fixed points in the semantic field. As a result, discomfort, update, criticality, and observation are unified into a single geometric structure.

This formulation shifts Future Convergence Theory from a temporal-causal interpretation toward a geometric theory of semantic tensor fields, providing a foundational step toward implementation in AI, cognition, and meaning-based systems.
