### Abstract



This paper provides a unified theoretical framework for explaining everyday anomalous experiences such as dreams, déjà vu, intuition, synchronicity, and near-death experiences. Rather than relying on speculative interpretations such as the many-worlds interpretation or time travel hypotheses, the study introduces a Double Fixed Point Theory consisting of a future fixed point and a simultaneous fixed point. This structure mathematically formalizes how the future’s semantic structure constrains present cognition, perception, and consciousness.

The theory integrates the future-conditional information inflow I, meaning density tensor M, projection operator \Pi, and state update mapping G, allowing these seemingly disparate phenomena to be derived from a single causal principle. Furthermore, it proposes empirically measurable indicators—such as the Dream Concentration Index (DCI), anticipatory overlap, future-conditional mutual information, and semantic closure—to enable the experimental investigation of future causality.

This work bridges physics, neuroscience, and everyday lived experience by explicitly formulating future causality as a unifying principle for time, information, and consciousness.
