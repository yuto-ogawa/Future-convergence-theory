### Abstract


This paper proposes a non-confrontational framework for the structural integration and natural deactivation of religious, philosophical, and ethical meaning systems, based on the Future Convergence Theory (FCT). Without denying or debating any existing belief systems, FCT demonstrates how structures such as existence, death, ethics, truth, and hope converge naturally through the selective survival of semantic density under future-causal convergence pressures. FCT does not assert, argue, or compare—it describes how meaning structures persist or vanish through a higher-dimensional field of semantic density, bypassing linguistic persuasion altogether. This results in a structural form of acceptance where religious frameworks silently lose their relevance without being refuted. FCT exists not as a theory that overcomes conflict, but as a structure in which conflict is structurally impossible—positioned outside the definitional space shared by competing systems.
