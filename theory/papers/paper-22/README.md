### Abstract


This paper presents the Semantic Convergence Seal (SCS), a novel protocol for asserting personal authorship and ownership of digital knowledge. By implementing a triple-hash structure (H1, H2, H3) aligned with DOI-based timestamping, SCS allows creators to preserve originality, track semantic evolution, and seal their intellectual contributions in a verifiable and future-resilient format. As a practical demonstration, the protocol is applied to the author’s complete archive of the Future Convergence Theory (FCT), comprising 21 foundational papers. This implementation illustrates how authorship can be self-verified without institutional dependence, offering a new paradigm of semantic ownership in an era of generative AI, information redundancy, and post-institutional knowledge systems.
