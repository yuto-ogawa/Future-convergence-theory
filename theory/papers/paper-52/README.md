### Abstract


This paper proposes a new theoretical and structural framework for ensuring the authenticity of video content in the era of rapidly advancing generative AI. Unlike static images, video inherently involves temporal continuity, intentionality, and observational traces, but AI-generated videos often lack these essential structures — leading to the disappearance of authorship, meaning, and temporal context.
 
To address this, the study formalizes irreproducible traces and semantic density flows left during human editing and creation processes as a future-reprojectable structure based on the Λ(T) framework. Authenticity is defined not through external verification but through the possibility of future meaning projection, turning each video into a structural record that can be verified from the future.
 
Furthermore, the paper introduces a three-layer architecture —
• FIP (Future Intent Projection),
• MTEA (Meaning Trace Embedded Auth), and
• SAEV (Semantic Auth-Embedded Video) —
to structurally embed meaning, traces, and cryptographic signatures into video content. This enables a shift from short-term consumption toward a future-oriented authenticity economy, where video carries lasting semantic and ethical weight.
