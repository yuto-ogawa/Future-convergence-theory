### Abstract


This paper aims to define the foundational preconditions preceding "Premise Update," a core operation in Future Convergence Theory (FCT). While conventional frameworks treat premise updating as a cognitive or selective process, this study redefines it as an irreversible structural transformation.

To formalize this transition, six prerequisite components are introduced: (1) dissonance detection mechanism, (2) self-non-exceptionalization condition, (3) meta-cognitive observation layer, (4) constraint coordinate system, (5) future alignment evaluation function, and (6) irreversibility degree of update.

Within this framework, the subject is no longer treated as a simple decision-maker, but as an active carrier of error detection, recursive observation, and temporal alignment. Consequently, premise updating is not an arbitrary cognitive act but a convergence process constrained by the future-causal operator Λ(T).

Furthermore, this paper demonstrates that deepening the implementation of the theory reveals a structural inseparability between the theory and the subject. This leads to the introduction of "authenticity" as a structural trace, where the validity of the theory is grounded not only in its formal content, but also in accumulated update history, responsibility linkage, and irreversible transformations.

Through this pre-definition, premise updating is reframed from a psychological operation into a temporal-structural transformation, providing a unified foundation across cognition, decision-making, ethics, and AI system design
