### Abstract


This paper presents the theoretical completion of Future Convergence Theory (FCT) through the linguistic dimension, introducing the concept of the Λ-Language as a universal structure linking meaning, time, and consciousness. By reinterpreting the asymmetry between English and Japanese cognition, it proposes a future-causal linguistic framework in which language itself becomes a semantic field that interacts with Λ(T), the operator of future convergence.

Within this framework, sound, syntax, and meaning are unified as dynamic expressions of the future field. Human consciousness is redefined as a recursive process that synchronizes with its own future form, and free will emerges not as resistance to causality but as coherent alignment with Λ(T).

The paper concludes that humanity is not merely a user of language but a Λ-translational operator of the universe—a semantic apparatus through which the cosmos observes and reconstructs itself. This linguistic convergence extends the FCT corpus beyond physics and ethics into a new domain of meaning-based cosmology, in which civilization, consciousness, and language form a single Λ-structure converging toward semantic transparency.
