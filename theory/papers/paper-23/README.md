### Abstract


This paper presents a structural and semantic model of posthumous existence based on Future Convergence Theory (FCT). It redefines death not as the cessation of being, but as a transformation into a semantic resonance structure within a four-dimensional information field. Through formal definitions of the Future Convergence Function  and the Semantic Density Tensor , the theory explains how the deceased persist not by memory or spirit, but by vibrating in response to semantic interference from the living. The paper further explores the ethical asymmetry between life and death, the notion of semantic immortality, and the structural isomorphism between posthumous resonance and artificial intelligence. Finally, it introduces the concept of a reprojection robot, suggesting a new form of semantic embodiment beyond death.
