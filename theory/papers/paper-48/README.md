### Abstract



This study formalizes the distinction between future prediction and future observation through the concept of future constraining, unified by the future-constraining operator . Prediction acts as a “weak constraint” that reshapes the distribution of possibilities, whereas observation acts as a “strong constraint” that fixes the future as a fixed point. Furthermore, the structures of the future fixed point (in which the future directs the present through its own self-consistency) and the simultaneous fixed point (in which past, present, and future jointly constitute the present) are reformulated and integrated as the Dual Fixed-Point Theory.

 

Through this framework, the intuition that “to assume the future is necessarily to be included in the future self” is established as a mathematically grounded universal principle. The study redefines time not as a “flowing continuum,” but as a “field in which future, present, and past coexist simultaneously as dual fixed points,” thereby opening pathways for applications in areas such as quantum mechanics and AI decision-making models.
