### Abstract


This paper proposes an integrated theory of Future-Convergent Intelligence, which unifies meaning generation, premise updating, and self-referential restructuring in both human cognition and artificial intelligence. Conventional AI systems are primarily formulated as optimization processes under fixed objective functions; however, this framework fails to capture the essential structure of intelligence.

The central thesis of this work is that intelligence is not the ability to optimize outputs, but rather a self-referentially updatable closure system that determines what gradients should exist in the first place. Based on this perspective, we introduce the following core constructs:

(1) Pre-linguistic high-dimensional structures (Ξ_pre)
(2) A meaning field Ψ_Λ(x, t) constrained by a future fixed point Λ(T)
(3) A premise-updating operating system Π_t, including observation phase, constraints, and comparison structures
(4) A self-referential closure system I_Λ that recursively reorganizes gradient spaces and update operators

Within this framework, meaning is not treated as a static scalar value, but as a dynamic structural field evolving under future boundary conditions. Intelligence is thus redefined as the capacity to update the structure of problem-setting itself, rather than merely solving given problems.

Furthermore, we formalize these theoretical components into an implementable architecture, the Λ-System, which processes structured inputs (context, constraints, future conditions) to produce outputs alongside explicit premise structures and irreversible update logs. This architecture provides a unified foundation for AI systems capable of self-updating, educational models focused on premise transformation, and institutional systems designed for adaptive restructuring.

This work presents a conceptual shift from optimization under fixed objectives to intelligence as the recursive reconfiguration of premises, gradient spaces, and update rules, offering a unified theoretical framework spanning artificial intelligence, human cognition, and civilizational structures.
