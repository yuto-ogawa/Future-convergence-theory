### Abstract


This paper extends Future Convergence Theory (FCT) by formalizing double fixed point theory and tri-temporal simultaneity as a unified basis for biological evolution and behavior generation. We model organisms as systems constrained by both a trace of the past (initial condition) and a fixed point of the future (goal/terminal condition), and we express online behavior through future-dependent recursion with well-posedness assumptions (bounded kernels, spectral radius < 1). The framework is applied to three distinct sensory-communicative modalities—whale song, pheromone communication in bees/ants, and human language—and shows that each can be captured by the same “future-referencing” structure via projection operators on an underlying information field \Phi. We define a tri-temporal operator that jointly processes past, present, and future signals, and we cast evolution as semantic convergence toward future stability conditions. The paper states a “Simultaneity Theorem of the Information Field,” introduces branching conditions for lineage-specific evolution, and discusses implications for human evolutionary singularities. This provides a mathematically coherent and biologically comparative account of future-causal evolution.
