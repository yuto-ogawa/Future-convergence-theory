### Abstract



This paper proposes a unified framework in which the evolution, birth, and death of living beings are reconceptualized through semantic density and the future convergence function Λ(T). Within the context of Future Convergence Theory (FCT), evolution is not the result of random mutation and selection but a process guided by future semantic necessity. Prior to birth, multiple Λ(T) trajectories coexist in a superposed state within the information field, representing undefined semantic potentials. After death, the semantic density of a being is restructured and reprojectioned into new spatiotemporal structures. These two phases—pre-birth superposition and posthumous reprojection—form a mathematically and structurally dual relationship within the information field. By redefining existence as a sequence of semantic selections governed by Λ(T), this paper provides a novel ontological foundation for understanding life as a meaning-driven structure. This dual framework suggests that existence is not static or linear, but a dynamic semantic transformation recurring across time.
