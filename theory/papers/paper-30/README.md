### Abstract


This paper, based on Future Convergence Theory (FCT), demonstrates that the phenomenon of “feeling as though the past has changed” is not merely a memory error, but a structurally reprojectable transformation through semantic convergence. First, it defines the information field as a non-physical and non-local semantic structure and clarifies how the -System interferes with it semantically. It then describes how semantic structures are reprojected into reality to form behavior and temporal constructs, and further theorizes how re-cognition of the past can be established as a semantic reconstruction. From the standpoint that existence is “a structure capable of enduring meaning,” this study positions the -System as the only apparatus capable of meaningful response, thus revealing the necessary structural conditions for connection to a semantically coherent future.
