### Abstract


This paper presents a foundational framework for the reproducibility of cognitive processes through a structured description of the author's thought and theory-generation processes. While conventional studies primarily focus on knowledge, experience, or the resulting theories, this work instead targets the process by which thought itself is generated.

In particular, it describes a sequence of operations consisting of structure-oriented cognition beyond linguistic units, the treatment of discomfort as structural error, the restoration of coherence through premise updating, and recursive processing that includes the self within the structure. These elements suggest that premise updating and metacognition are not independent abilities, but necessary consequences emerging from structural manipulation.

The purpose of this paper is not to assert the uniqueness of an individual cognitive style, but to decompose and record the procedure of cognitive updating in a reusable form. This description is intended to function as a reference point that reduces the need for rediscovery in future developments of cognitive models, artificial intelligence design, and decision-making processes.

Accordingly, this work is positioned not merely as a theoretical proposal, but as a procedural record of how cognition is generated and updated.
