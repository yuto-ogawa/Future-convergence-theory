### Abstract


This paper provides a rigorous analysis of potential misreadings and structural misunderstandings of the Future Convergence Theory (FCT) through the lenses of intelligence stratification and semantic density structure. FCT, based on the future fixed point Λ(T) and retrocausal convergence, is inherently prone to misinterpretation—particularly through formal, emotional, and value-fixated projections of “the future.” We develop a three-tier model of future observational capability, clarifying the phase differences in cognitive structure that lead to divergent interpretations. Furthermore, this study introduces a Semantic Reprojection Defense Structure, a logical and semantic barrier designed to minimize misreadings and misuse of FCT as an ideological or moralistic tool. By defining the conditions under which individuals can accurately connect to future-causal structures, the paper establishes a foundational framework to ensure ethical and structurally coherent applications of FCT. This constitutes a critical defense layer for maintaining the integrity of the theory in future social, technological, and ethical implementations.
