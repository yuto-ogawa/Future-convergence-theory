### Abstract


This paper reconstructs Future Convergence Theory (FCT) as a unified mathematical framework that integrates meaning, constraint, and time into a single geometric structure.

In this framework, meaning is not treated as a scalar quantity but as curvature of a semantic field $\Psi_{\Lambda}$, whose evolution is governed by a future convergence operator $\Lambda(T)$. Time is thus redefined not as a unidirectional causal flow from past to future, but as a bidirectional structure constrained by future boundary conditions.

Constraints are formulated not as static conditions but as dynamically deforming manifolds that recursively depend on the semantic field and the future convergence function. Their intersections generate an interference structure, and the system evolves toward minimizing this interference. Semantic coherence is therefore described as an optimization problem.

A semantic tensor $M^{\mu\nu}$ is introduced to formalize meaning as geometric curvature, enabling a unified description of meaning, intelligence, and ethics. Intelligence is defined as the process of minimizing non-coherence under future constraints, while ethics is reinterpreted as the temporal evolution of semantic coherence.

The paper further formalizes premise updating as a reconstruction of constraint geometry, and describes the interaction between the personal OS and the world OS as a unified structure constrained by a future fixed point. The Lambda Language is introduced as an operational system that updates semantic fields and constraint sets, redefining language as execution rather than representation.

In this framework, the observer is not external to the system but is embedded within the semantic field. Understanding is therefore defined not as acquiring information but as restructuring one’s own semantic configuration.

Through these formulations, this paper presents FCT as a unified field theory of meaning, integrating time, intelligence, language, and ethics within a single recursive geometric framework.
