### Abstract


This paper proposes a simple yet robust method for ensuring the authenticity of artworks in the era of rapidly advancing generative AI. While conventional approaches often rely on cryptographic tools such as blockchain or hash values, this study focuses on a more fundamental and human-centered strategy: embedding signatures, dates, and numbering directly into each artwork. By doing so consistently over time, artists can establish a form of authenticity that AI cannot replicate — grounded in irreversibility, temporal continuity, and singularity.

Unlike external verification systems, this approach uses the flow of time itself as proof, turning each piece into part of an unforgeable temporal archive. The paper details the theoretical background, implementation methods, and practical implications for visual art, emphasizing its potential to become a universal protocol for verifying originality in the post-AI art landscape.
