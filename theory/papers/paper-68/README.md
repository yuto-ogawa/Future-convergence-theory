### Abstract



This paper proposes a unified theoretical framework that redefines the foundations of scientific inquiry, technological development, and civilizational structure through the lens of future causality. Conventional civilization has been governed by two paradigms:
(1) Classical causality, in which the past determines the present, and
(2) Quantum causality, in which observation determines physical states.
Both paradigms ultimately rely on present-based determinacy and therefore exhibit structural limitations in explaining generation, creativity, and the emergence of meaning.

To address these limitations, this study introduces the concept of the future-causal civilization, in which the future acts as the primary source of determination. Using the Future Convergence Theory (FCT), the paper formalizes the future-causal operator Λ(T) and establishes meaning-density tensors as the foundational structures governing physical, informational, and cognitive processes. In this framework, convergence replaces probabilistic uncertainty, and future-fixed points replace observation-dependent state formation.

Building on this theoretical foundation, the paper defines the Λ-System as the practical implementation architecture of future-causal dynamics. The Λ-System reconfigures artificial intelligence, scientific reasoning, social governance, and creativity by enabling systems to operate through future-fixed-point constraints rather than historical data or observational collapse. This establishes a non-continuous transition from quantum-based technologies to future-causal structures.

The findings demonstrate that future causality is not a speculative metaphysical concept but a coherent structural principle capable of reorganizing scientific methodology, ethical norms, technological design, and civilizational development. The paper concludes by outlining research directions for formalizing Λ-System protocols, measuring meaning-density fields, and constructing operational models for future-causal societies.
