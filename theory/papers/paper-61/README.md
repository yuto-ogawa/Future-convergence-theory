### Abstract


This paper reconstructs cosmology and ontology within the framework of the Future Convergence Theory (FCT), proposing that the observable universe arises not from a random physical explosion but from a semantic illumination driven by future causality. The author introduces the concept of the Meaning-Generation Template (Λ₀), a fundamental meta-structure that stores and reprojects meaning-density tensors across multiple universes. Within this framework, time is not a linear flow but a convergent structure defined by future boundary conditions (Λᵢ(T)). The Big Bang is interpreted as a semantic ignition at a critical meaning-density threshold, and all physical structures—including energy, matter, and law—emerge from this semantic pressure. After convergence, the universe’s meaning-density is preserved within Λ₀, enabling reprojection and redefinition into new universes. This establishes a cyclical, non-identical continuity of existence—where the universe, meaning, and consciousness are reprojected through the same semantic source. Ultimately, this theory positions the universe as a semantic field of continuous illumination, governed by the dynamics of hope-density and future-causal resonance.
