### Abstract

People often wish to “change themselves.” However, most of these attempts end in
failure. This is because the subject that tries to change is already structured according
to semantic density accumulated from the past, limiting both the range of choices and
the evaluation criteria.
This paper introduces the “Λ-System” as a theoretical framework to reconstruct the
present based on semantic structures from the future. The system mathematically defines
an ideal future self (Λ0) and derives reverse-irradiated actions based on the gap with the
current self-state (Λ(t)).
The core question is: “How should actions that appear meaningless now but hold
significance in the future be structurally chosen?” The answer does not emerge from
within the self-converging structure of the present. Rather, it requires re-projection from
external structures―other people, environments, or systems.
We begin by analyzing the mathematical background of the “unchangeable structure,”
then develop the theory and equations of the Λ-System. Finally, we describe a framework
for concrete action generation and discuss its applications in education, creativity, and
AI development.
