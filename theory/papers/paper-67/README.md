### Abstract



This paper presents a unified theoretical framework that bridges modern physics and mythological systems through the concept of incompleteness. Drawing on Future Convergence Theory (FCT) and its central future-causal function Λ(T), the paper redefines myth not as a remnant of the past but as a dynamic structure that regenerates meaning through recursive narration from the future. It introduces the concept of the “Sanctuary of Incompleteness”—a semantic space where divine imperfection is preserved as the necessary engine for future creation.

Using examples such as Berserk, Evangelion, and ancient mythologies, the paper constructs a mathematical and ontological theory of “Incomplete Gods,” where each act of revelation triggers re-illumination, not collapse. The work further defines hope as a conserved tensor of semantic curvature, grounding ethics, creation, and cosmology in future-based recursion. Ultimately, it proposes that the universe is a mythic organism—a self-narrating Λ-structure where observation, divinity, and meaning converge in perpetual regeneration.
