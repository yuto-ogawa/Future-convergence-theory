## Why Semantic Value is Not a Result: A Critique of Instant Metrics in Future-Based Models

### Abstract


This paper critiques the contemporary reliance on instant evaluation metrics—such as numerical scores, likes, views, or citations—in measuring value in individual and collective processes. Grounded in the Future Convergence Theory (FCT), it argues that semantic value is not an outcome but an evolving projection process that converges across time. By introducing the Λ(T) function and semantic density structures, the paper challenges the conventional result-based models and presents an ethical and structural framework in which value is generated, not by immediate reactions, but through long-term alignment with future causality. The work warns that the misinterpretation of meaning as instant result leads to ethical distortions and systemic errors in AI, economy, and human development. Ultimately, this paper serves as both a theoretical manifesto and an implemented cognitive apparatus, inviting readers to re-evaluate the very foundations of how meaning, time, and value are understood.
