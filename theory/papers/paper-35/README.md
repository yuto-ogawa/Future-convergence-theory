### Abstract



Corruption in education arises when the system becomes optimized for external evaluation, reducing the
essence of a“question”to immediate indicators such as scores and correct answers. The Λ-System counters
this by taking the fundamental stance that“a question is not something to be evaluated, but a dynamic
manifestation of semantic density.”
This paper redefines the structure of“questions”from the perspectives of education, evaluation, and
ethics, describing how the Λ-System functions as an apparatus that shifts from“questions for evaluation”
to “questions for reconstruction.”
Particularly important is the paradox that evaluating a question in itself indicates“a lack of design.”
The value of a question arises structurally, and once value is imposed externally, the question is already
dead. Therefore, in the Λ-System, no scoring is applied to the question itself; instead, it focuses solely on
the structural background in which the question arose, the dynamic changes in the semantic tensor, and the
traces of its reconstruction.
This paper portrays, from the perspective of the system’s designer, how the Λ-System maximizes human
semantic density while maintaining a consistent principle of“never evaluating a question,”across the redesign
of education, semantic understanding in AI, structures of political agreement, and structural freedom of the
individual.
