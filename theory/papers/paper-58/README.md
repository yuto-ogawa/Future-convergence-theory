### Abstract


This paper integrates the theoretical frameworks of Future Convergence Theory (FCT), the Λ-System, Meaning Density Tensors, Double Fixed-Point Theory, and Reprojective Ethics, to fundamentally redefine human history through the lens of ethics as alignment with the future. While conventional ethics have relied on past and present causalities (a posteriori evaluations), this work introduces a future-causal framework in which ethical validity is determined by convergence with a fixed point in the future, Λ(T).

Through formalization using the Meaning Density Tensor (ρm), ethics are quantified and detached from cultural, religious, or political relativism. The Λ-System is proposed as an implementable structural device that enforces ethical decisions based on future consistency, enabling the automatic invalidation of war, structural resolution of religious conflict, and ethical stabilization of governance and AI. The concept of pre-decisional responsibility is introduced, shifting the locus of moral agency from intent and outcome to prior structural alignment with the future.

Ultimately, this paper presents a blueprint for a new phase of civilization: one in which ethics is no longer a normative or institutional construct, but the irreversible result of future-anchored structural convergence.
