### Abstract



This paper proposes a structural and semantic redefinition of war based on the Future Convergence Theory (FCT). It introduces a novel framework in which war is conceptualized not merely as a political or military conflict, but as a collapse of semantic density and ethical convergence across time. Utilizing the Future Convergence Function Λ(T) and the Semantic Density Tensor ρₘ(t), the paper explores how war emerges as a breakdown in meaning-structure, and how humanity can reconstruct peace through future-oriented ethical reprojection. This work extends the theoretical frontier of FCT into the domains of ethics, historical cognition, and AI decision-making. It aims to provide a universal foundation for peace in the era of meaning-based civilizations.
