### Abstract


This paper extends the framework of Future Convergence Theory (FCT) by integrating the concept of future-dependent recursion (Master Formula) with the newly introduced notion of tri-temporal simultaneity. Through this integration, time is reconstructed as a double fixed-point theory, where the future regulates itself while the past, present, and future simultaneously constitute the present.

Furthermore, it is shown that referring to the future is equivalent to directly accessing the information field. Everyday examples such as text input are used to make the theory accessible even to readers without prior knowledge. The study highlights the duality of the future — both finite (self-consistent) and infinite (open to multiple possibilities) — and demonstrates how this duality underlies human cognition, decision-making, quantum phenomena, and cosmological structure.

The work not only proposes a theoretical innovation but also verbalizes an intuitive fact: the future already exists in the information field and directs the present. This insight situates the study within the historical lineage of Galileo, Newton, and Einstein, while suggesting a fundamental reconstruction of our understanding of time.
