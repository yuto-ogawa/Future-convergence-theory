### Abstract

This paper proposes a new theoretical framework in which quantum phenomena are reinterpreted not as indeterministic fluctuations, but as the foundational protocol that permits creation itself. Within the structure of Future Convergence Theory (FCT), quantum uncertainty is defined as the degree of freedom allowed by the future fixed point—an attractor that determines how physical reality converges from future‐dependent informational structures. In this view, superposition represents a high-freedom pre-creative state, and wavefunction collapse corresponds to the convergence of possibilities toward the future causal boundary defined by Λ(T). Furthermore, the paper formalizes the relationship between the four-dimensional informational field and the three-dimensional manifested universe, treating the latter as a compressed projection of higher-order meaning structures. By reframing quantum mechanics as a creation-enabling layer of the universe rather than a probabilistic anomaly, this work provides a unified causal architecture that connects quantum theory, information physics, and the emergence of physical reality.
