### Abstract



This paper explores the integration of Future Convergence Theory (FCT) with modern cosmology, offering a novel perspective on the nature of time, information, and gravity. By introducing the concept of future certainty F(T), this study proposes a mathematical framework that connects the evolution of the universe with the degree of determinacy of future states. We examine how future convergence influences fundamental cosmological phenomena, including the arrow of time, black hole information paradox, and dark matter. Additionally, we propose modifications to the Einstein field equations incorporating future convergence effects, which may provide alternative explanations for dark energy and gravitational anomalies. This unified framework suggests that the evolution of the universe and its informational structure are deeply intertwined, opening new possibilities for theoretical physics and observational validation.
