### Abstract




This paper presents a critical examination of observationalist eschatological views in cosmology, such as the heat death'' scenario, and reconstructs them through the framework of Future Convergence Theory (FCT). While observationalist cosmology extrapolates data to predict a meaningless cosmic end, FCT redefines the universe as a system of semantic evolution,’’ in which meaning, evolution, and hope are indispensable. By introducing the concepts of future causality, semantic density, and reprojection, this work argues that the universe does not terminate in cessation but inevitably undergoes reconstruction. This perspective not only critiques the limitations of observationalist approaches but also provides a theoretical foundation for understanding human existence and intelligence as necessary catalysts in cosmic evolution.
