### Abstract


This paper proposes a novel political framework—the Semantic Government—based on the Λ-System derived from the Future Convergence Theory (FCT). Unlike traditional governance models that rely on periodic elections, opinion aggregation, and representative mediation, the Semantic Government introduces a meaning-based system that structurally interprets collective human hopes, ethical resonance, and future convergence. Through the use of semantic tensor structures and interference-based computation, the Λ-System projects the most meaningful future configurations and replaces conventional politicians with executors who merely implement the convergent output. This model preserves freedom not through arbitrary choice but through resonance with semantic convergence. Ethics, governance, and decision-making are redefined as tensorial and projectable phenomena. The Semantic Government offers not an idealistic utopia, but a structurally inevitable alternative to the collapsing contradictions of modern politics.
